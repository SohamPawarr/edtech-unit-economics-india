## Project Overview

This is an end-to-end consulting engagement simulation on a fictional **Series B EdTech startup — EduVista** — operating in the IIT-JEE coaching segment in Tier 2 India.

**The crisis:** EduVista is burning ₹3.2 Cr/month with only 6 months of runway and a LTV/CAC ratio of 0.8x — far below the industry minimum of 3:1. The business model is structurally broken.

**The engagement:** Diagnose root causes, evaluate three strategic pivot options, build a month-by-month financial model, and deliver a structured recommendation using the SCR framework — exactly as a strategy consultant would.

## Frameworks Applied

### 1. SCR Framework *(McKinsey Consulting Standard)*
Structures the entire narrative as **Situation → Complication → Resolution** — the backbone of every slide in the deck.

### 2. Unit Economics Decomposition
Full breakdown of:
- **LTV** = ARPU × Gross Margin % × Avg Lifetime Months
- **CAC** = Total Acquisition Spend ÷ New Students Acquired
- **CAC Payback Period** = CAC ÷ (ARPU × Gross Margin %)
- **Contribution Margin** = Revenue − Variable COGS − Variable OpEx

## Root Cause Analysis

Three structural failures identified:

1. **CAC at 4.5× the viable threshold** — over-reliance on paid digital acquisition with no referral flywheel
2. **58% churn by Month 6** — no offline touchpoints, low perceived accountability in pure-online model
3. **Unscalable cost structure** — live faculty costs scaling linearly with students, no leverage from recorded content

---

## The Recommendation

> *Option B — Phygital Lite — is the only option that solves all three root causes simultaneously within the existing 6-month runway without requiring external capital.*

- Deploys **5 weekend study hubs** in Tier 2 cities (Indore, Patna, Nagpur, Lucknow, Jaipur) at ₹80K/city/month
- Shifts to **recorded content + live doubt sessions** → faculty cost drops from ₹2.1 Cr to ₹0.9 Cr/month
- Activates **peer referral program** at study hubs → CAC reduces from ₹18,000 to ₹10,800 by Month 3
- Achieves **contribution margin breakeven at Month 5** with 4,500 enrolled students
